Overview

A sheltered space that still feels outdoors

A glass room gives you cover overhead while keeping the area light and airy. It’s ideal when you want to reduce wind and make the space more usable, without closing everything in. The result is a clean, premium extension of the property that makes outdoor time easier in more conditions.

If you want a roof that fully retracts for open sky, see Retractable roof systems. If you want adjustable shade and airflow, see Bioclimatic louvre pergolas.

Options

Add comfort and control

Many glass room projects are improved with side protection and privacy, especially on exposed patios and terraces. Terrace screens are a popular addition to reduce wind and create a more comfortable feel.

If your priority is quick shade rather than a structure, you may prefer an Awning. For open areas where you want flexible cover, see Giant umbrellas or Pagodas and canopies.

For homes

A glass room makes the patio feel calmer and more comfortable. It’s ideal for outdoor dining, relaxing, and creating a space you can use more often, even when the breeze picks up. It also keeps the area bright, which helps the space feel like a natural part of the home.

For hospitality and commercial terraces

For restaurants, pubs, hotels and venues, keeping outdoor areas comfortable helps protect covers and improves the guest experience. A glass room gives a premium look, clear shelter, and a space that feels inviting without feeling boxed in.

If you need a larger flexible structure, explore Retractable roof systems. For wide open areas where you want adaptable cover, explore Giant umbrellas.

Questions

FAQs

Do you install across the UK?

Yes. We are based in the South West and install nationwide.

Do glass rooms block light?

No. Glass rooms are designed to keep the space bright while providing shelter overhead.

Do I need planning permission?

It depends on the size, structure and location. We can provide guidance and recommend checking with your local council for larger installations.

Can I add privacy and wind protection?

Yes. Terrace screens are a popular addition for exposed areas and extra privacy.

What is the best alternative if I want more airflow control?

Bioclimatic louvre pergolas give adjustable airflow and shade. Retractable roof systems give open-sky flexibility.
