Overview

What a bioclimatic pergola gives you

A bioclimatic pergola uses adjustable louvres to manage light and ventilation. Angle them for shade, open them to let heat escape, or close them when you want protection. The result is a space that feels comfortable more often, without losing the open-air feel.

If you want a roof that fully retracts for open sky, see Retractable roof systems. If you want a brighter, more enclosed option, see Glass rooms.

Options

The upgrades that make the biggest difference

The best pergola setups are the ones that match how you use the space. Many customers choose to enhance comfort and privacy, especially on exposed patios and terraces.

For wind protection and privacy, add Terrace screens. If you want a softer shade option in a different style, Sail shade solutions can be a great alternative.

If your main priority is quick shade without a full structure, an Awning may be the right fit.

For homes

A louvre pergola makes outdoor time easier. You can create shade for lunch outside, reduce glare through patio doors, and keep the area comfortable even when the sun moves. It also helps you stay outside longer when conditions are changeable, without making the space feel closed in.

For hospitality and commercial terraces

For restaurants, pubs, hotels and venues, comfort keeps guests outside. A bioclimatic pergola gives you a clean, premium structure with day-to-day flexibility. Angle the louvres to manage sun on warm days, then close them for cover when the weather turns.

For larger open areas where you want flexible coverage without a permanent structure, explore Giant umbrellas or Pagodas and canopies.

Questions

FAQs

What does “bioclimatic” mean?

It refers to controlling the microclimate of the space by managing shade and ventilation. The adjustable louvres help you balance sun, airflow and shelter.

Do you install across the UK?

Yes. We are based in the South West and install nationwide.

Do I need planning permission?

It depends on the size, structure and location. We can provide guidance and recommend checking with your local council for larger installations.

Can I add side protection for wind and privacy?

Yes. Terrace screens are a popular addition for exposed areas and extra privacy.

What is the best alternative if I want a fully opening roof?

If you want the roof to retract for open sky, see Retractable roof systems. If you want a bright enclosed space, see Glass rooms.
